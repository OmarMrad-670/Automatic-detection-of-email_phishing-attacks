Phishing email detection refers to the process of identifying and recognizing fraudulent emails that are designed to deceive recipients and steal their personal information. By carefully examining various elements of an email, such as the sender's address, the email content, and any embedded links or attachments, individuals can spot indicators of phishing attempts. These may include suspicious email addresses, generic greetings, spelling or grammatical errors, urgent or threatening language, requests for personal information or financial details, and mismatched URLs. Detecting phishing emails is crucial for maintaining online security and protecting oneself from potential cyber threats.
